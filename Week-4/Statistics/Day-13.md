# 2. POPULATION AND SAMPLE

Statistics often studies a subset of data instead of the entire data.

---

## Population

### Definition

A population is the complete collection of all individuals, objects, or observations of interest.

### Example

If we want to study the height of all students in a college:

**Population = All students in the college**

---

## Sample

### Definition

A sample is a smaller group selected from the population for analysis.

### Example

**Sample = 200 students selected from the college**

---

## Population vs Sample

| Feature  | Population    | Sample               |
| -------- | ------------- | -------------------- |
| Size     | Entire Group  | Subset of Population |
| Cost     | High          | Low                  |
| Time     | More          | Less                 |
| Accuracy | More Accurate | Approximation        |

---

## Values Used

### Population Parameter

A numerical measure describing a population.

Examples:

* Population Mean (μ)
* Population Standard Deviation (σ)

---

### Sample Statistic

A numerical measure describing a sample.

Examples:

* Sample Mean (x̄)
* Sample Standard Deviation (s)

---

# 3. AREAS OF STATISTICS

Statistics is mainly divided into two branches.

---

## 1. Descriptive Statistics

### Definition

Descriptive Statistics summarizes and presents data in a meaningful way.

### Activities

* Tables
* Charts
* Graphs
* Mean
* Median
* Mode

### Question Answered

> What happened?

### Example

Monthly sales report showing average sales.

---

## 2. Inferential Statistics

### Definition

Inferential Statistics uses sample data to make conclusions about a population.

### Activities

* Hypothesis Testing
* Confidence Intervals
* Predictions
* Estimation

### Question Answered

> What can be concluded about the population?

### Example

Predicting election results from a sample survey.

---

## Comparison

| Feature | Descriptive      | Inferential      |
| ------- | ---------------- | ---------------- |
| Purpose | Summarize Data   | Draw Conclusions |
| Uses    | Charts, Averages | Predictions      |
| Data    | Available Data   | Sample Data      |
| Output  | Reports          | Decisions        |

# 4. BASIC TERMS IN STATISTICS

---

## Population

Entire collection of observations.

### Example

All employees in a company.

---

## Sample

Subset selected from a population.

### Example

100 employees surveyed.

---

## Variable

A characteristic that can take different values.

### Examples

* Age
* Height
* Salary

---

## Data (Singular)

The singular form is **Datum**.

Example:

One observation = Datum

---

## Data (Plural)

A collection of observations.

Example:

Student marks dataset.

---

## Random Variable

A variable whose value depends on the outcome of a random experiment.

### Examples

* Tossing a coin
* Rolling a dice

---

## Experiment

An activity that produces observable outcomes.

### Examples

* Coin Toss
* Dice Roll
* Survey

---

## Statistic

A numerical value calculated from sample data.

### Examples

* Sample Mean
* Sample Median
* Sample Standard Deviation

---

# 3. SAMPLING

### Definition

Sampling is the process of selecting a subset of individuals from a population.

---

## Types of Sampling Based on Selection

---

### 1. Sampling With Replacement

An item selected can be selected again.

### Example

Selecting a card, recording it, and placing it back before selecting again.

```text
Select
   ↓
Record
   ↓
Replace
   ↓
Select Again
```

---

### 2. Sampling Without Replacement

Once selected, the item cannot be selected again.

### Example

Lottery Draw

```text
Select
   ↓
Record
   ↓
Remove
   ↓
Next Selection
```

---

## Comparison

| Feature         | With Replacement | Without Replacement |
| --------------- | ---------------- | ------------------- |
| Reuse Possible  | Yes              | No                  |
| Independence    | Independent      | Dependent           |
| Population Size | Constant         | Decreases           |

# 6. TYPES OF SAMPLING

Sampling is mainly classified into:

```text
Sampling
│
├── Probability Sampling
│
└── Non-Probability Sampling
```

---

# A. PROBABILITY SAMPLING

Each member of the population has a known probability of selection.

---

## Types of Probability Sampling

### 1. Simple Random Sampling

Every member has an equal chance of selection.

Example:

Lottery Method

---

### 2. Systematic Sampling

Select every nth item.

Example:

Every 10th customer.

---

### 3. Stratified Sampling

Population divided into groups (strata).

Example:

Students divided by department.

---

### 4. Cluster Sampling

Population divided into clusters.

Randomly selected clusters are studied.

Example:

Selecting 5 schools from a district.

---

# B. NON-PROBABILITY SAMPLING

Selection is based on judgment or convenience.

---

## Types of Non-Probability Sampling

### 1. Convenience Sampling

Easily available participants.

Example:

Surveying nearby students.

---

### 2. Judgment Sampling

Researcher selects participants.

Example:

Expert Interviews.

---

### 3. Quota Sampling

Fixed number selected from groups.

Example:

50 males and 50 females.

---

### 4. Snowball Sampling

Existing participants recruit new participants.

Example:

Rare disease studies.

---

# COMPARISON OF SAMPLING TYPES

| Feature        | Probability | Non-Probability |
| -------------- | ----------- | --------------- |
| Selection      | Random      | Non-Random      |
| Bias           | Less        | Higher          |
| Accuracy       | High        | Moderate        |
| Generalization | Better      | Limited         |
